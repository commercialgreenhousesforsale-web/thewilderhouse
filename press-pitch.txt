THE PITCH

In 1898, a Savannah grocer named John Hinson Wilder built a Victorian duplex on East
Park Avenue — not as an investment, not for commerce, but out of a father's love for
his family. He wanted his children side by side, close but with lives of their own.
He chose one of Savannah's finest streets, three blocks from Forsyth Park, and he
built something meant to last.

It has lasted 125 years.

In 1998 — exactly one hundred years later, to the year — another father drove down
East Park Avenue and made the same decision. The building had four units. He had four
children. He bought it so each of them would have something lasting of their own.
A father's love, expressed in brick and heart-pine on the same address where a
stranger had done the same thing a century before.

Neither father could have known the other. But the building remembers both of them.

THE FULL STORY

Today, The Wilder House at 314-316 East Park Avenue is a luxury vacation rental in
Savannah's Victorian District — three blocks from Forsyth Park, rated 4.97 stars
across 209 verified guest stays, Airbnb Superhost and Guest Favorite.

Travis — born at the old Telfair Hospital three blocks away, raised and schooled in
this neighborhood, trained as a carpenter and HVAC technician, and a web developer
who built this property's entire digital presence himself — hosts both suites
personally.

But the story behind the building is unlike anything else in American hospitality.

John Wilder's son, Reverend John Stephen Wilder, was raised in this home and became
known throughout Savannah as "Savannah's Pastor" — a man who sat beside the accused
in local courtrooms to advocate for ordinary citizens, who conducted 6,817 funerals,
made 146,158 sick visits, and preached 6,239 sermons over 52 years of service at
Calvary Baptist Church. The house on East Park Avenue was where his story began.

Travis's story began three blocks away. His father purchased this building for Travis,
his two brothers, and his sister — one unit for each child — so they would have
something real to build their futures on. Travis spent years learning the building
from the inside out: the carpentry, the systems, the history, the responsibility of
keeping something genuine alive in a city that knows the difference.

He has hosted guests for three years. Superhost every year. 4.97 stars. 209 reviews.

Two fathers. One corner. One hundred years apart. The same love.

That is what this building is made of.
