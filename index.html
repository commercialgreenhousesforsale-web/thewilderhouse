Historic Forsyth Park Home Built with Love Since 1898

John Hinson Wilder — a grocer who worked just down the street — built this duplex in 1898 as a gift for his family. Two private entrances, side by side, so his children could be close but with lives of their own. That logic still holds. 127 years later, the original heart-pine floors, the 11-foot plaster ceilings, and the address itself remain exactly as he chose them.

Soaring 11-foot ceilings. Original heart-pine floors that creak just enough to remind you you’re staying in a real historic home, not a replica. Fully stocked kitchens, private off-street parking, and a picnic basket — for the 4-minute walk to Forsyth Park.

Two of downtown Savannah’s only grocery stores are three blocks from the front door. 314 East Park Avenue sits on the street that runs along the park’s east side — one of the closest licensed vacation rental addresses to Forsyth Park in Savannah.

Forsyth Park Location: Why This Savannah Vacation Rental Wins

Savannah’s real magic isn’t on crowded River Street. It lives in the moss-draped streets of the Victorian District surrounding Forsyth Park — and we sit right in the middle of it.

  • 🌳

    Forsyth Park — 4-Minute Walk

    30 acres of live oaks, the iconic cast-iron fountain, a fragrance garden, walking paths, tennis courts, two playgrounds for kids, and the Saturday farmers market. Three blocks and four minutes from the front door.

  • ☕

    The Sentient Bean

    The best lavender latte in Savannah is 5 minutes on foot. The Sentient Bean sits right at the park’s edge — coffee on a bench under a live oak is a reasonable morning plan.

  • 🌿

    Saturday Farmers Market — 3 Blocks

    Forsyth Park hosts one of Georgia’s finest farmers markets every Saturday morning — fresh flowers, local produce, and artisan food steps from your front door.

  • 🏛

    Victorian District Architecture

    Dozens of preserved 19th-century homes surround you. Attend the Jazz Festival or simply wander every gorgeous street.
