Why Savannah Rewards the Budget Traveler

You Can Have a Perfect Day in Savannah for $0

Most of what makes Savannah unforgettable is free. The city's whole layout — James Oglethorpe's 1733 grid of public squares — was designed to be walked, and it still is. You don't pay to stand under the live oaks, to watch the fountain at Forsyth Park, to ride the river ferry, or to wander Bonaventure Cemetery at golden hour. The expensive parts of a Savannah trip are the optional ones: paid tours, sit-down dinners, and parking.

This is a local's list, kept honest. Where something is genuinely free, we say so. Where it's free to enter but costs a little nearby — or free to look at but charges admission inside — we tell you that too. And because the best free thing to do changes by the weather, the tide and the hour, the list ends with the live tool we built to pick it for you in real time.

The List · What's Actually Free

14 Genuinely Free Things to Do in Savannah

01

Forsyth Park & the 1858 Fountain

Always Free

Savannah's 30-acre signature park, anchored by the cast-iron fountain modeled on the one in Paris's Place de la Concorde. Walk the oak-lined paths, find the fragrance garden, watch the fountain turn gold in late light. Three blocks from the Victorian District. Open daily, no admission.

02

Walk the 22 Historic Squares

Always Free

All 22 squares from the 1733 plan survive. Start at Forsyth and work north to the river — Chippewa (the Forrest Gump bench scene), Monterey (Mercer-Williams House), Lafayette (the Cathedral), Madison, Johnson. Budget about three hours. This self-guided walk is the single best way to see Savannah, and it's free.

03

Bonaventure Cemetery

Always Free

Garlands of Spanish moss over Victorian monuments, four miles east. Free to walk or drive through, open dawn to dusk. Go at golden hour (4–6pm) when the light filters through the oaks. Johnny Mercer and Conrad Aiken are buried here; Aiken's bench-shaped headstone invites you to sit.

04

Colonial Park Cemetery

Always Free

Savannah's oldest cemetery, in use from 1750, gated right in the middle of downtown on Abercorn Street. Free to enter and read the centuries-old headstones under the trees — a quiet stop between squares.

05

River Street & the Riverwalk

Always Free

Cobblestone streets along the working river, lined with shops and galleries. Free to stroll, and the cargo ships pass within yards of the walk. Best at sunset, and the start point for the free ferry below.

06

The Free DOT Shuttle

Always Free

Two free loops — Forsyth and Downtown — covering River Street, City Market, the squares and the History Museum. Every 10–15 minutes, 7 days a week, no ticket. Just wave it down. Most tourists never find out about it. Routes at catchacat.org.

07

Savannah Belles Ferry

Always Free

A free passenger ferry between River Street and Hutchinson Island, every 15–20 minutes into the evening. Ride it across at dusk and watch the skyline light up on the water — one of the most romantic free things in the city, and almost nobody knows it's free.

08

Cathedral Basilica of St. John the Baptist

Free to Visit

Savannah's twin-spired French Gothic cathedral on Lafayette Square. Free to step inside between Mass times — the painted interior and stained glass are among the most beautiful in the South. Be respectful of services; donations welcome, not required.

09

Forsyth Park Farmers Market

Free Entry

Saturdays, 9am–1pm, south end of Forsyth Park, year-round. Free to enter and browse local produce, bread, flowers and prepared food. You only spend if you buy — and grabbing a pastry to eat in the park is the cheapest great morning in town.

10

First Friday Art Walk & SCAD Galleries

Free Entry

On the first Friday of each month, galleries open their doors along Broughton Street with free openings. The SCAD galleries (Pei Ling Chan, Alexander Hall and others) are free to enter year-round — a real, rotating art scene at no cost.

11

Live Jazz in the Squares

Always Free

Free outdoor jazz on weekend evenings in Johnson and Wright Squares. Bring a drink — Savannah's open-container law makes it legal to carry one — and listen under the oaks for nothing. One of the best free things to do in Savannah at night.

12

Plant Riverside — Generator Hall

Free to Walk In

The public hall inside the Plant Riverside District is free to walk through, and home to "Atlas," a glittering full-size dinosaur skeleton, plus a gem-and-fossil gallery. A genuinely odd, genuinely free indoor stop — great on a rainy day or a hot afternoon.

13

Tybee Island Beach

Free Beach · Paid Parking

A free public beach 18 miles east, about 25 minutes by car. The sand and the surf cost nothing — only parking is paid (bring quarters or the app). Pack a cooler and you've got a free beach day. No direct public transit, so you'll need a car or rideshare.

14

Broughton Street & an Architecture Walk

Always Free

Window-shop Savannah's main retail street, then let the buildings be the attraction: antebellum mansions, ironwork balconies, haint-blue porch ceilings. The whole Historic District is an open-air museum you can tour for free, at your own pace.

A note on the “almost free” ones: Wormsloe's famous avenue of 400 oaks is $10 to enter — worth it, but not free. Leopold's Ice Cream isn't free, but a scoop of the 1919 rum bisque is a few dollars and a Savannah rite of passage. And tipping your free tour guide or buskers, while optional, is the decent thing to do.

Free Things to Do in Savannah — FAQ

Is Savannah expensive to visit?

No — Savannah is one of the most affordable beautiful cities in the South because so much of what makes it special is free: the squares, the parks, the cemeteries, the architecture, a free shuttle and a free ferry. You can spend a full, memorable day downtown without paying for a single attraction. The main costs are food, paid tours and parking, all easy to minimize.

Is Forsyth Park free?

Yes. Forsyth Park is a free, public 30-acre park open daily. The 1858 cast-iron fountain, the walking paths, the fragrance garden and the Saturday farmers market are all free to enjoy.

Is there free parking or transport in Savannah?

The free DOT shuttle runs two downtown loops every 10–15 minutes, seven days a week, no ticket needed, and the Belles Ferry across the river is free. For cars, residential streets south of Gaston Street are often free after 6pm and on Sundays, and the Chatham County Courthouse garage on Montgomery Street is among the cheapest downtown.

Can you visit Bonaventure Cemetery for free?

Yes. Bonaventure Cemetery is free and open daily from dawn to dusk — walk or drive through at no cost. The most beautiful light is golden hour, roughly 4–6pm on a clear day, when the sun filters through the live oaks and Spanish moss.

What free things are there to do in Savannah at night?

Walk Bull Street and the lit squares from Forsyth Park to the river, catch free weekend jazz in Johnson and Wright Squares, ride the free Belles Ferry across the river to watch the skyline light up, and stroll River Street. Open container lets you carry a drink the whole way, which keeps a night out nearly free.

What's free to do in Savannah when it rains?

On a rainy day the free options include the Cathedral Basilica between Masses, the free SCAD galleries, the public Generator Hall at Plant Riverside with its dinosaur skeleton, and window-shopping the covered storefronts on Broughton Street. The free DOT shuttle keeps you dry between stops.
