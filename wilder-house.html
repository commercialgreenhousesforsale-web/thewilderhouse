Built in 1898 for Family. Hosted for Guests Today.

John Hinson Wilder — a grocer who worked just down the street — built this home in 1898 out of a father’s love for his family — a Victorian duplex so his children could be side by side, close but with lives of their own. More than 125 years later it is still standing on East Park Avenue, still welcoming guests, still three blocks from the heart of Forsyth Park. Some buildings just have that in them.

Today The Wilder House is two vacation rental suites — Suite 314A and Suite 316A — inside the same 1898 building, on the same East Park Avenue. The spirit John Wilder built into it is still here. Guests mention it without being able to fully explain it. The address, 314 East Park Avenue, sits three blocks from Forsyth Park’s east entrance — on the street that runs along the park’s edge, among the closest licensed vacation rental addresses to the park in Savannah’s Victorian District.
