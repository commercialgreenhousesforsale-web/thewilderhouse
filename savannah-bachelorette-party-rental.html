The Location

Savannah’s Most Beautiful Neighborhood — Four Minutes from the Park

The Victorian District is the quieter, more residential side of Savannah’s historic core — Spanish moss, preserved 19th-century mansions, and the kind of streets that make you stop to take photos before you even reach Forsyth Park. It is the best neighborhood in Savannah for a bachelorette party that wants the city’s beauty without being in the middle of its tourist crowds.

  • 🌳

    Forsyth Park — 4 Minutes on Foot

    The most iconic park in Savannah. The 1858 cast-iron fountain is three blocks from the front door — perfect for morning walks, afternoon fountain photos, and the Saturday farmers market.

  • 🍟

    Bars & Restaurants — 5–10 Minutes

    Bull Street’s cocktail bars and restaurants are 5 minutes on foot. Broughton Street shopping and nightlife is 10–15 minutes. Collins Quarter — the best coffee in Savannah — is 5 minutes away.

  • 🚌

    Free DOT Shuttle to River Street

    The free DOT purple shuttle runs to River Street and City Market with a stop nearby. Get to the waterfront bars and restaurants without splitting Uber costs across the whole group.

  • 🏛

    Victorian Architecture — Everywhere

    Every street in this neighborhood is a bachelorette photo opportunity. Preserved 19th-century homes, iron fences, and the most beautiful tree canopy in the American South surround the property on all sides.

Bachelorette Rental FAQ

Common Questions

What does booking both suites include for a bachelorette group?
When you book both suites, your bachelorette group gets four bedrooms (two king beds and two queen beds), two full bathrooms, two fully stocked kitchens, a shared private deck with gas grill and pergola, and two dedicated off-street parking spots — all inside the same 1898 building at 314-316 East Park Avenue. Suite 314A and Suite 316A are side by side. A party of up to 8 has the whole building to themselves — no strangers next door, no hotel hallways.
How many guests does the bachelorette rental sleep?
The full bachelorette rental sleeps up to 8 guests across four bedrooms — two king beds and two queen beds, one of each per suite. The double booking is designed for a group of 5 to 8. A party of 4 or fewer may find a single suite a better fit and value.
Is Savannah a good destination for a bachelorette group?
Savannah consistently ranks among the top bachelorette destinations in the American South. The city offers a walkable historic downtown with world-class Southern dining, rooftop bars, ghost tours, and river cruises. The Victorian District — where The Wilder House is located — gives your group an upscale, quieter base while keeping everything walkable or a short shuttle ride away. Use the Savannah Day Planner to see what’s on during your dates.
What is there to do near The Wilder House for a bachelorette group?
Forsyth Park is 4 minutes away — ideal for morning walks and photos at the 1858 fountain. Bull Street bars and restaurants are 5–10 minutes on foot. Broughton Street shopping and nightlife is a 10–15 minute walk. River Street, City Market, and the waterfront are accessible via the free DOT purple shuttle. Ghost tours, riverboat cruises, cooking classes, and spa days are all within easy reach by rideshare.
Do both suites have their own kitchen?
Yes — both Suite 314A and Suite 316A have fully stocked kitchens with stainless steel appliances, butcher block counters, full cookware, coffee maker, dishwasher, microwave, and ice maker. Suite 314A has the maroon island; Suite 316A has the haint blue island with an original 1898 fireplace in the adjacent living room. Two kitchens means your group can prep brunch, cocktails, and late-night snacks simultaneously without fighting over counter space.
Are two parking spots included?
Yes. Each suite includes one dedicated off-street parking spot at no extra charge. When your group books both suites, you have two private spots. In the Victorian District, where street parking is limited and competitive on weekends, having two guaranteed spots is a genuine advantage — especially when returning late from a night out.
How does check-in work for a large bachelorette group?
Both suites have smart lock self check-in with their own access codes, sent before your arrival. Your group can arrive in separate cars at different times and check into each suite independently. Check-in is at 4:00 PM and checkout at 11:00 AM. Both the front entrance and rear entrance are accessible on both suites — no coordinating, no waiting, no wake-up calls.
