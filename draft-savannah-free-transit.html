Savannah's two free rides, in plain English

The city's free transit is run by Chatham Area Transit and it comes in two parts. Neither one costs a cent, and you never need a ticket or an app to ride.

🚌 The dot shuttle — two loops through the Historic District

Small buses run two loops, about every 10 minutes. The Forsyth loop runs up Drayton Street and down Whitaker, connecting Forsyth Park with the squares and City Market. The Downtown loop circles the upper Historic District — Congress, Liberty, City Market — out to Eastern Wharf on the river. They meet near Liberty Street, so you can transfer between them.

Hours: Monday–Friday 7am–7pm, Saturday & Sunday 10am–6pm (lighter hours on some holidays; closed Thanksgiving, Christmas, and New Year's Day).

⛴ The Savannah Belles Ferry — across the river, free

Four little ferries — each named for a famous Savannah woman — cross the Savannah River between River Street and Hutchinson Island every 10 minutes, from 7am to 10pm, every day. Landings: City Hall and Waving Girl on the River Street side, and the Convention Center (by the Westin) on Hutchinson Island. It takes bikes, luggage, and leashed dogs — and the ride is a genuinely lovely (free) view of the waterfront.

When the free ride is worth it — and when to just walk

Honest truth: the Historic District is small and made for walking, and most of the squares are a 5–10 minute stroll apart. The free shuttle earns its keep when it's hot, when it's raining, when feet are tired, or for the longer hops — Forsyth Park up to River Street, or anything out to Eastern Wharf. The ferry is the easy way to the Convention Center and hotels on Hutchinson Island (and a fun little cruise for free). This planner will tell you honestly when walking is just as fast.

Getting to Tybee, Bonaventure & Wormsloe

The free shuttle stays downtown — it does not reach Tybee Island, Bonaventure Cemetery, Wormsloe, or Skidaway. Those need a car or rideshare. (For River Street from the Forsyth Park area, though, the free shuttle is perfect.)
