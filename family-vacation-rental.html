Family Rental FAQ

Family Vacation Rental FAQ

Is The Wilder House good for families with young children?

Yes. Self-contained suites with a full kitchen, two separate bedrooms, and a private entrance make it easy to keep to your family’s routine. Forsyth Park’s open lawns and iconic fountain are 4 minutes away on foot. Smart lock check-in means you arrive on your schedule.

Is private parking free and guaranteed?

Yes — one dedicated off-street parking spot is included with every booking at no extra charge. In the Victorian District, where street parking is metered and competitive, having a guaranteed spot when you arrive with kids and gear is a genuine advantage.

What is Forsyth Park like for families?

Thirty acres of shaded lawns centered on the 1858 cast-iron fountain — free to enter, dog-friendly, and 4 minutes from the front door on foot. The Saturday farmers market brings fresh produce, local food vendors, and a neighborhood atmosphere that families love.

Does the kitchen have everything we need?

Yes. Both suites have fully stocked kitchens with stainless appliances, full cookware sets, a dishwasher, and a coffee maker. Everything you need to cook family breakfast, pack a picnic, or keep snacks on hand without eating every meal out.

Our family is larger than 4 — can we all stay here?

Book both suites together for 4 bedrooms, 2 full kitchens, 2 bathrooms, and 2 parking spots — sleeping up to 8. The suites are side by side in the same 1898 building, each with a private entrance. Ideal for extended families or two families traveling together. From $395/night.

Is the Victorian District safe and walkable with kids?

Yes. The Victorian District is one of Savannah’s most established residential neighborhoods — quiet streets, well-maintained historic homes, and easy walking to Forsyth Park, Bull Street restaurants, and Leopold’s Ice Cream. Families walk everywhere without needing to move the car.

How far is Forsyth Park from the front door?

Four minutes on foot. 314 East Park Avenue is three blocks east of Forsyth Park along East Park Avenue. The park’s east entrance opens directly to the fountain. No driving, no parking garage.

What else can families do within walking distance?

Two of downtown Savannah’s only grocery stores are three blocks away — the most practical thing families mention after the parking. Leopold’s Ice Cream, Broughton Street shopping, the Savannah History Museum, and River Street are all reachable without a car. The free DOT purple shuttle stops nearby and runs 7 days a week to City Market and River Street.
