Welcome to The Wilder House

A Legacy Built on Love & Southern Grace

Local grocer John Wilder built this home in 1898 as a heartfelt gift for his children — a sanctuary of elegance and family connection. More than 125 years later, it still carries that warm, loving spirit.

Soaring 12-foot ceilings. Original heart-pine floors that creak just enough to remind you you’re in a real historic home. Fully stocked kitchens, private off-street parking, and a picnic basket — ready for Forsyth Park in under two minutes.

Savannah’s True Heart — On Your Doorstep

Savannah’s real magic isn’t on crowded River Street. It lives in the quiet, moss-draped streets surrounding Forsyth Park — and we sit right in the middle of it.

  • 🌳

    Forsyth Park — 2.5 Blocks Away

    30 acres of walking paths, tennis courts, the famous fountain, and Saturday farmers market. Under those live oaks in under 2 minutes.

  • ☕

    Collins Quarter & Artisan Cafes

    The best lavender lattes in Savannah are a 5-minute stroll away. World-class dining at every turn.

  • 🛒

    Kroger Grocery — 3 Blocks Away

    Stock your fully stocked kitchen on arrival. Kroger is three blocks away — easy and convenient.

  • 🏛

    Dozens of preserved 19th-century mansions surround you. Attend the Jazz Festival or simply wander every gorgeous street.
