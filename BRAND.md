# THE WILDER HOUSE — OFFICIAL BRAND POSITIONING

**Last Updated:** May 23, 2026  
**Brand Status:** Locked & Live

---

## CORE BRAND VALUES
**LOVE. HOME. HISTORY.**

- **LOVE** — Built by John Hinson Wilder as a gift to his family (1898), not commerce. That founding love infuses the space.
- **HOME** — First and foremost, this is a home. Guests stay in a sanctuary. A protected place.
- **HISTORY** — Genuine 1898 Victorian quadplex. Authentic. Original heart-pine floors, 11-foot ceilings. Not a replica.

---

## PRIMARY BRAND — HOMEPAGE HERO

### Headline
**"The Wilder House. Historic Home. Forsyth Park."**

### Subheader
**"Built out of love in 1898. Still carries that spirit."**

### Why This Works
- **"Historic Home"** = Emotional core + authenticity (not just "luxury")
- **"Built out of love"** = Leads with intention, not features
- **"Still carries that spirit"** = Continuity of founding values
- **"Forsyth Park"** = Location benefit + neighborhood prestige

## TARGET GUEST PROFILE

**Who Books The Wilder House:**
- Affluent travelers (willing to plan ahead)
- Seek authenticity over luxury logos
- Value emotional connection with places
- Appreciate real history, not theme-park versions
- Want to feel welcomed & safe (not just accommodated)
- Understand scarcity & book early

**What They're Looking For:**
- Genuine historic experience

- Personal touch (not corporate)

## BRAND VOICE & TONE

**Do:**
- Poetic, literary language
- Personal & warm
- Specific historical details
- Emotional appeals (safety, belonging, memory)
- Elegant but not stuffy

**Don't:**
- Corporate jargon ("maximize your experience")
- Hype language ("luxury," "premium" without context)
- Generic vacation rental copy
- Anything that feels commercial or impersonal
